## Month 1: May 2026 — Launch the Thread

### Post 1: "The 50-Year-Old Computer Model That Predicted 2025"
- **Hook:** In 1972, a team at MIT ran a computer simulation of civilization's future. Fifty years later, their model is tracking reality with uncomfortable accuracy.
- **Key data:** World3 dashboard screenshot showing BAU scenario vs. real-world population and industrial output overlaid. RMSE fit scores for each scenario.
- **CTA:** Link to live dashboard. "Play with it yourself."
- **Expert to ping:** Gaya Herrington (her 2021 KPMG/Yale paper validated the same model — this dashboard builds on her work). Tag on LinkedIn + X.

### Post 2: "Which Collapse Scenario Are We Actually On?"
- **Hook:** The Limits to Growth had four scenarios. Only one avoids collapse. The data says we're not on that one.
- **Key data:** Dashboard grid view showing all 4 scenarios (BAU, BAU2, CT, SW) with "You Are Here" markers. Highlight that real data tracks BAU/BAU2 most closely.
- **CTA:** "Here's what each scenario assumes — and what it would take to switch tracks."
- **Expert to ping:** Florian Jehn (Existential Crunch Substack — he covers civilizational resilience and has engaged with World3 literature).

## Month 3: July 2026 — Deep Dives

### Post 5: "The Resource Problem Nobody Talks About"
- **Hook:** Everyone debates oil and rare earths. The World3 model says the real constraint is how fast we deplete ALL nonrenewable resources simultaneously — and we're ahead of schedule.
- **Key data:** Dashboard nonrenewable resources chart. BAU vs. BAU2 resource depletion curves with real extraction data overlay.
- **CTA:** "Double the resources only buys 20 years. Here's the math."
- **Expert to ping:** Simon Michaux (Geological Survey of Finland — quantitative resource depletion research).

### Post 6: "Technology Won't Save Us (According to the Model That's Been Right for 50 Years)"
- **Hook:** The CT (Comprehensive Technology) scenario assumes we deploy every technological solution aggressively. It still ends in overshoot — just 30 years later.
- **Key data:** CT vs. SW scenario comparison from dashboard. Show that CT delays but doesn't prevent collapse; only SW (full policy bundle) stabilizes.
- **CTA:** "Technology is necessary but not sufficient. Here's what else the model requires."
- **Expert to ping:** Herrington again (her paper explicitly discusses CT vs. SW implications for policy).

## Month 4: August 2026 — Policy and Real-World Connections

### Post 7: "What 'Stabilized World' Actually Requires"
- **Hook:** The only World3 scenario that avoids collapse requires four simultaneous policy shifts starting in 2002. We missed that deadline. What does that mean?
- **Key data:** SW scenario parameter breakdown — population stabilization, industrial output caps, resource efficiency, pollution controls. Compare to actual policy trajectory.
- **CTA:** "The window is narrowing, but the model parameters tell us exactly what levers to pull."
- **Expert to ping:** Jorgen Randers (original Limits to Growth co-author, still active — has published on "what would it take" scenarios).

### Post 8: "The Food-Population-Pollution Triangle"
- **Hook:** World3 predicted that food per capita would peak and then decline as pollution degrades agricultural land. FAO data shows food production is still rising — but the rate is slowing.
- **Key data:** Dashboard food per capita overlay. Show model prediction vs. FAO kcal/cap/day data. Discuss where the curves might cross.
- **CTA:** "We're in the good part of the curve. Here's what happens next."
- **Expert to ping:** Florian Jehn (food system resilience is one of his core topics).
