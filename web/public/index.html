Where Are We on the Limits to Growth?

The World3 computer model from The Limits to Growth (Meadows et al., 1972) projected several possible futures. This dashboard overlays those scenarios with real-world data from public sources (World Bank, NOAA, Our World in Data) so you can see — variable by variable — which path humanity is actually following.

How to read this

Series are normalized to 1970 = 1.0 so the abstract World3 model units can be compared with real measurements. The overlay shows directional alignment, not absolute calibration.

Real-world proxies follow Herrington (2021): GDP/cap stands in for industrial output, kcal/cap/day for food, CO₂ + plastic for pollution, mean years of schooling for services.

Methodology & caveats

The World3 model used here is the original 1974 implementation via pyworld3. Real-world series are normalized to 1970 = 1.0 to align units; the comparison is directional, not absolute calibration.

Variable proxies (Herrington 2021):

  • Industrial output / capita — GDP per capita (constant 2015 USD), World Bank.
  • Food per capita — daily kcal supply, FAO via Our World in Data.
  • Persistent pollution — CO₂ ppm (NOAA Mauna Loa) plus annual plastic production (OWID), composite index.
  • Services per capita — mean years of schooling (UNDP via OWID).
  • Population — direct, World Bank.

Caveats: mean-years-of-schooling has sparse coverage; the pollution composite mixes a stock (CO₂) with a flow (plastic). World3 was never intended for point-prediction — evaluate trajectory shape, not single-year levels.

Full methodology: docs/METHODOLOGY.md.

Sources: World Bank Open Data, NOAA GML, Our World in Data, FAO, UNDP. Model: pyworld3 (Meadows et al., 1972). Methodology after Herrington (2021) and Nebel et al. (2024).
